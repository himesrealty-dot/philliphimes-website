The Brittany Lakes Experience

The hidden gem on League City's west side — and the people who find it rarely leave

"Brittany Lakes doesn't shout about itself, and that's exactly why residents love it. Three lakes, mature trees, trails, great schools, and one of the lowest HOAs in the area — it's everything you want without the premium price tag."

Developed across 14 phases beginning in the late 1990s, Brittany Lakes was built around its most defining feature: water. Three lakes wind through the community, the largest supporting kayaking, canoeing, and catch-and-release fishing. Ducks and geese are as much a part of the neighborhood as the neighbors themselves.

Two-plus decades of growth have given Brittany Lakes something newer communities simply don't have yet — a mature tree canopy, settled landscaping, and a neighborhood identity that residents describe as genuinely close-knit. Kids grow up here. Families stay for a second decade. There's a reason it's one of the most searched established communities on League City's west side.

The value proposition is real: a community pool with splash pad, tennis court, multiple playgrounds, parks with picnic areas, miles of walking trails, and three lakes — all for an HOA among the lowest in League City's established master-planned inventory. For buyers who want quality, character, and CCISD schools without overpaying for the zip code, Brittany Lakes consistently delivers.

Common Questions

Brittany Lakes League City — frequently asked questions

What school district is Brittany Lakes in?
Brittany Lakes is fully within Clear Creek ISD (CCISD). Every home in the community feeds into League City Elementary, League City Intermediate, and Clear Creek High School. CCISD is consistently rated one of the top school districts in the Houston Bay Area — and Brittany Lakes' 100% CCISD status means no split-district confusion. Always confirm the specific school assignment for any address before purchasing.
What are the HOA fees in Brittany Lakes?
Brittany Lakes HOA fees are among the lowest in League City's established communities — approximately $550–$865 per year (roughly $46–$72 per month). Fees cover the community pool and splash pad, recreation center, tennis court, three lakes, parks, playgrounds, and common area maintenance. Visit brittanylakes.org or verify the current amount for your specific section before closing, as fees can vary slightly.
What builders built homes in Brittany Lakes?
Brittany Lakes was developed across 14 phases beginning in the late 1990s, featuring homes by Perry Homes, Village Builders, Gehan Homes, Plantation Homes, Kimball Hill Homes, David Weekley Homes, and others. This mix of quality regional builders across the 2000–2010 construction era gives the community genuine variety in floor plan styles, sizes, and price points.
What is the price range for homes in Brittany Lakes?
Home prices in Brittany Lakes range from approximately $300,000 for entry-level homes (1,800–2,500 sq ft) to $700,000 or more for premium lakefront properties. The most active market is the $400,000–$500,000 range. The median sale price typically hovers between $370,000 and $415,000, making Brittany Lakes one of the most affordable established communities in League City relative to its amenities and school access.
How far is Brittany Lakes from downtown Houston?
Brittany Lakes is approximately 25–30 minutes from downtown Houston via I-45 North — one of the shorter League City commutes thanks to its west-side location near the freeway. NASA Johnson Space Center is about 15 minutes away, UTMB League City is roughly 20 minutes, and Kemah Boardwalk is about 20 minutes.
Does Brittany Lakes have lakes you can use?
Yes — Brittany Lakes has three community lakes. The largest supports kayaking, canoeing, and catch-and-release fishing. The lakes are home to local wildlife year-round including ducks and geese, and are connected to the community's trail system. This is one of the features that most distinguishes Brittany Lakes from other League City neighborhoods at its price point.
What restaurants are near Brittany Lakes?
Popular nearby dining includes Legends Sports Grill and Red Oak Cafe (both at 6011 W Main St), Kabuki Japanese Grill & Sushi Bar (2525 Gulf Fwy S), Jimmy Changas (2504 S Gulf Fwy), The Toasted Yolk Cafe (2535 Gulf Fwy S), and Rudy's Country Store & Bar-B-Q (21361 Gulf Fwy, Webster). The Gulf Freeway corridor puts a wide range of options within minutes.
What grocery stores are near Brittany Lakes?
H-E-B (2955 Gulf Fwy) is close and is the go-to for most residents. Kroger (1920 W League City Pkwy) is also nearby with a full pharmacy and fuel center. CVS and Walgreens cover pharmacy and convenience needs. Most Brittany Lakes residents can complete their grocery run in under 10 minutes.
What amenities does Brittany Lakes have?
Brittany Lakes features a community pool with splash pad, a recreation center, a tennis court, three playgrounds, parks with picnic areas, and three lakes with kayaking, canoeing, and catch-and-release fishing. Miles of tree-lined walking trails and open green spaces connect the entire community. All of this is covered by one of the lowest HOA fees in League City's established neighborhood inventory.
What is it like to live in Brittany Lakes?
Residents consistently describe Brittany Lakes as peaceful, family-oriented, and genuinely community-minded. It's the kind of neighborhood where people wave from the trail, kids learn to kayak in the lake, and neighbors know each other's names. The mature tree canopy, low traffic, and natural setting give it a character that's become increasingly rare in fast-growing South Houston suburbs. For families who value quality of life, outdoor recreation, and CCISD schools at a realistic price point, Brittany Lakes is hard to beat.
