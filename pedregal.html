The Pedregal Experience

New construction with land — a combination this market almost never offers

"Everywhere else in the Bay Area, new construction means a small lot and a two-story squeezed next to its neighbor. Pedregal flips that — sprawling one-story homes on oversized lots, with garage space most neighborhoods can't touch."

Pedregal is one of the newest communities in the Dickinson area — built by Cervelle Homes from 2021 onward at the League City–Dickinson line, near FM 517 and the Lobit Education Village. The formula is simple and rare: single-story floor plans from roughly 2,200 to 3,700 square feet on estate-size lots, with long driveways and oversized garages — 3-, 4-, even 6-car capacity shows up routinely in the MLS here.

The community is built around a pond with walking trails rather than dense amenity packages — quiet streets, room between neighbors, and a low-key pace. One housekeeping note for buyers: most Pedregal addresses carry a League City mailing address with the 77539 zip, but the neighborhood is zoned to Dickinson ISD. It's a border community in the best sense — League City convenience, Dickinson schools, Galveston County taxes.

And the market has noticed: over the past six months, not a single Pedregal listing failed to sell — every home either closed, went pending, or is still actively marketed. In an area where roughly a third of listings die on the vine, that's a statement about demand for this product.

Common Questions

Pedregal — frequently asked questions

What school district is Pedregal in?
Pedregal is zoned to Dickinson Independent School District (DISD). The typical feeder pattern runs through Dunbar Middle School and Dickinson High School. The specific elementary campus assignment can vary by street — always confirm before purchasing.
Is Pedregal a waterfront community?
No — despite the coastal-sounding name, Pedregal is an inland new-construction community. Its water feature is a community pond with walking trails, and a handful of lots have pond views. There are no canals or boat docks in Pedregal. For true canal or bay access in the Dickinson area, ask Phil about waterfront options along Dickinson Bayou and Dickinson Bay.
What is the price range in Pedregal?
Home prices in Pedregal range from approximately $410,000 for roughly 2,200 sq ft single-story plans to about $710,000 for the largest homes at 3,500+ sq ft. The most active band is $445K–$560K, and the recent median sale is about $495,000 based on the last six months of MLS closings.
How far is Pedregal from Galveston?
Pedregal is approximately 22 minutes from Galveston Island via I-45 South — close enough for regular beach days and UTMB medical appointments without paying Galveston Island property prices.
What are the HOA fees in Pedregal?
Pedregal HOA fees are $450 per year. Always verify the current amount with the HOA or community association before closing.
How do Galveston County taxes compare to Harris County?
Galveston County property tax rates are generally lower than comparable Harris County addresses — often $1,000–$2,000 per year less on similarly priced homes. This financial advantage is one of the primary reasons buyers choose Dickinson over closer-in Houston suburbs. Always verify current rates with the Galveston Central Appraisal District.
