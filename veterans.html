The VA home loan benefit isn't just a program — it's a competitive edge that most veterans significantly underutilize. Here's what it actually gives you.

Full purchase price financed with no down payment required for eligible veterans with full entitlement. Conventional loans typically require 5–20% down.

No PMI
PMI can cost $100–$200+ per month on a conventional loan. VA loans eliminate it entirely — that's real money back in your pocket every month.

Your VA benefit isn't single-use. As long as you pay off or sell the previous home, entitlement is restored. You can use it again and again throughout your life.

Built In
VA appraisals include minimum property requirements that protect you from buying a home with major deficiencies. The VA works for the veteran — not the seller.
Know Your Benefit

The Questions Every Veteran Should Ask Before Buying

The VA loan has nuances. Know these before you start — they affect your strategy.

What's the VA funding fee and who pays it?
The VA funding fee is a one-time fee (typically 2.15% for first-time use with $0 down) that replaces the mortgage insurance conventional borrowers pay monthly. It can be financed into the loan. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating are exempt from the funding fee entirely.
Can I use my VA benefit if I still have a VA loan on another property?
Yes, in some cases. If you have remaining entitlement, you may be able to use a second VA loan. The calculation depends on your current loan balance, the county loan limit, and whether you've restored entitlement. We'll run this before you commit to anything.
Do sellers in this market resist VA offers?
Some sellers have misconceptions about VA loans — longer timelines, stricter appraisals. A well-prepared VA offer with an experienced agent can win against conventional offers. I know how to present VA offers so sellers take them seriously.
What does the VA appraisal mean for negotiations?
If the VA appraisal comes in below the purchase price, you have options: negotiate the price down, pay the difference in cash, or walk away. The VA appraisal protects you — you can't be forced to pay more than appraised value without a choice.
Can I use BAH (Basic Allowance for Housing) to qualify?
Yes. BAH is considered non-taxable income, and lenders typically gross it up by 25% for qualifying purposes — meaning your BAH goes further toward your debt-to-income ratio than the same dollar amount of taxable income would.
How do I get my Certificate of Eligibility (COE)?
Your COE confirms your entitlement and is required before you can use the VA loan. It can be obtained through the VA website, your lender (many can pull it directly), or through a VA-approved agent. We handle this early in the process so nothing slows you down.
What property types qualify for a VA loan?
Primary residences only — single family homes, townhomes, condos (VA-approved), and some multi-family (if you occupy one unit). Investment properties and vacation homes don't qualify. New construction requires specific VA approval for the builder.
What if I've had a bankruptcy or foreclosure?
The VA has more lenient guidelines than conventional loans. Typically 2 years post-bankruptcy discharge and 2 years post-foreclosure — though timing and circumstances matter. Don't assume you don't qualify. Let's look at your specific situation.
