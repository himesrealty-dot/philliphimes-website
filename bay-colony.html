The Bay Colony Experience

An established master-planned community — League City address, Dickinson ISD value

"Bay Colony is one of those neighborhoods buyers underestimate until they drive it — mature trees, community lakes and parks, and a League City address that's still zoned to Dickinson ISD with Galveston County taxes."

Bay Colony is a large, established master-planned community built out mostly between 1998 and 2019. It's an inland neighborhood — there are no canals, no boat docks, and no Galveston Bay access. What it does have is real community infrastructure: a network of manmade community lakes (a few sections are literally named "Lakes in Bay Colony" and "Centerfield Lakes in Bay Colony"), neighborhood parks, and the kind of mature landscaping you only get from a community that's been here a couple of decades.

It's also genuinely big. Sections like Bay Colony Pointe, Pointe West, Northpointe, Parkside, Meadows, Bentwood, and the Enclave at Bay Colony West mean there's an unusually wide selection of floor plans and price points under one community umbrella — handy when you want a specific size or layout and don't want to compromise on the area.

The location quirk is the selling point: Bay Colony sits inside League City city limits but is zoned to Dickinson ISD, and it's in Galveston County. So you get a League City address, Dickinson ISD schools, and Galveston County property taxes that generally run lower than comparable Harris County addresses. That combination is hard to find, and it's why buyers keep landing here.

Homes in Bay Colony

Established resale homes across many sections and price points

Bay Colony is a built-out resale community of established single-family homes, built primarily between 1998 and 2019. Most sales fall between $210K and $400K, with a median around $295K. The wide range of sections — from Bay Colony Pointe to the Enclave at Bay Colony West — means there's a lot of choice in size, layout, and price.

Common Questions

Bay Colony — frequently asked questions

What school district is Bay Colony in?
Bay Colony sits inside League City city limits but is zoned to Dickinson ISD, with the typical feeder pattern of Bay Colony Elementary → Dunbar Middle School → Dickinson High School. School campus assignments are address-specific — always confirm the specific assignment for any address before purchasing.
Is Bay Colony a waterfront community?
No. Bay Colony is an established inland master-planned community — there are no canals, no boat docks, and no direct Galveston Bay water access. Its water features are manmade community lakes (some sections are named Lakes in Bay Colony and Centerfield Lakes in Bay Colony), along with neighborhood parks. A limited number of lots back to or overlook those community lakes.
How much do homes in Bay Colony cost?
Most homes in Bay Colony sell between $210,000 and $400,000, with a median around $295,000. Entry-level homes start in the low $200Ks, the bulk of the market sits in the $275K–$340K range, and larger or updated homes reach the upper $300Ks to $400K. A few oversized 4,500+ sq ft homes have closed in the mid-$500s, but those are rare. Always confirm current pricing for any specific home.
When were the homes in Bay Colony built?
Bay Colony was built primarily between 1998 and 2019 across many sections — including Bay Colony Pointe, Pointe West, Northpointe, Parkside, Meadows, Bentwood, Enclave at Bay Colony West, and Lakes in Bay Colony. It is a built-out, resale community with essentially no new construction.
What are the HOA fees in Bay Colony?
HOA dues in Bay Colony vary by section and MUD, so there is no single figure for the whole community. Always confirm the exact HOA amount for any specific home before closing.
What are Galveston County property taxes like?
Bay Colony is in Galveston County, whose property tax rates are generally lower than comparable Harris County addresses — often $1,000–$2,000 per year less on a similarly priced home. Always verify current rates and any MUD taxes with the Galveston Central Appraisal District.
