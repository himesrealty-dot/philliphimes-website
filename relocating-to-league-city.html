Why League City

What Relocating Families Find When They Arrive

League City is one of the fastest-growing cities in Texas — and the families who move here rarely leave. Located 25 miles southeast of downtown Houston, it sits at the intersection of everything that matters: top-rated schools, master-planned communities, easy freeway access, no state income tax, and a cost of living well below national averages.

For families relocating from California, the Pacific Northwest, or the Northeast, the value proposition is immediate and obvious. Homes that would cost $1.5M+ in California cost $400K–$700K here — with more square footage, better lots, and HOA-managed amenities. For families transferring for NASA JSC, the Texas Medical Center, or the Port of Houston, League City puts you 10–25 minutes from work instead of an hour.

Phil Himes has helped dozens of relocating families navigate every aspect of the move — from virtual tours and remote offers to school zone verification and neighborhood matching. He knows which communities fit which buyer profiles, and he'll tell you the honest truth about flood zones, HOA quality, and resale trajectories.

Cost of Living

Your Dollar Goes Significantly Further

League City's cost of living is consistently below the national average — and dramatically below major coastal metros. The combination of no state income tax, lower housing costs, and affordable property taxes makes it one of the best value relocations in the country.

Property taxes run approximately 2.0–2.5% of assessed value annually — higher than California's Prop 13 baseline, but offset entirely by the income tax elimination for most households. Phil can walk you through a full cost comparison for any scenario.

Is League City TX a good place to live?

Yes — consistently. League City combines top-rated schools, safe communities, easy access to major employers (NASA, TMC, Energy Corridor), no state income tax, and Gulf Coast proximity. It's one of the most sought-after suburbs in the entire Gulf Coast region for good reason.

How far is League City from downtown Houston?

About 25 miles — 30-35 minutes under normal traffic via I-45. It's closer to the Texas Medical Center (~25 min) and dramatically closer to NASA's Johnson Space Center (~10 min).

Can I buy a home in League City remotely?

Yes — Phil works with relocating buyers regularly. He conducts live video tours, provides honest neighborhood commentary, and coordinates remote closings. Many of his relocation clients have purchased without visiting in person until moving day.

What school district is League City TX in?

Most of League City is served by Clear Creek ISD — one of the highest-rated school districts in Texas, with an A rating from the TEA and five recognized high schools. Some western portions are served by Dickinson ISD. Phil verifies school zone for every address.

What is the cost of living in League City TX?

Below the national average. No state income tax. Median home price ~$375K as of 2026. Property taxes run 2.0–2.5% of assessed value — higher than some states, but the income tax elimination offsets this for most households. Grocery, utility, and dining costs are all moderate.

Is League City TX safe?

Very — League City is one of the safest cities of its size in Texas. It consistently earns top safety rankings among Greater Houston suburbs. Master-planned communities with active HOAs and a strong community culture contribute to a low-crime environment.
