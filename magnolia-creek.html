The Magnolia Creek Experience

A private golf community built into the fabric of the neighborhood — not just next to it

"In most communities, the golf course is a feature. In Magnolia Creek, it's the entire setting. Fairways and greens are your backyard view, the clubhouse is your social calendar, and the pace of life is exactly what you moved here for."

Magnolia Creek was designed from the ground up as a golf community — not a neighborhood that happens to have a course nearby. The private 27-hole Magnolia Creek Golf Club runs through the entire development, giving fairway and green-space views to a significant portion of the homes. The result is a natural, resort-feel setting that's rare at this price point in the Houston market.

Development began in the early 2000s with a master plan that paired Perry Homes, Lennar, and K. Hovnanian Homes with a variety of traditional and Mediterranean-influenced floor plans. Build-out was largely complete by 2012–2016, giving the community a mature, established character with good tree cover and well-settled landscaping throughout its sections.

Beyond the golf, Magnolia Creek functions as a fully featured master-planned community — resort-style pools, walking trails, playgrounds, parks, and creeks and green spaces woven through the neighborhood. For families, the fully CCISD school assignment to Clear Springs High School is a significant draw. For active residents, the optional club membership unlocks golf, Lone Pine Grill dining, tennis, fitness, and one of west League City's most active social scenes.

Common Questions

Magnolia Creek League City — frequently asked questions

What school district is Magnolia Creek in?
Magnolia Creek is fully within Clear Creek ISD (CCISD). Every home in the community feeds into Gilmore Elementary, Victory Lakes Intermediate, and Clear Springs High School. Clear Springs is consistently ranked among the top high schools in the greater Houston area. Always confirm the specific assignment for any address before purchasing.
What are the HOA fees in Magnolia Creek?
Magnolia Creek HOA fees are approximately $726 per year (around $60/month). This covers community pools, playgrounds, parks, walking trails, and common area maintenance. Golf club membership is separate and optional — Magnolia Creek Golf Club is a private club. Membership unlocks full access to the 27-hole course, Lone Pine Grill, tennis courts, and fitness center. Always verify the current HOA fee for your specific section before closing.
Is the golf course open to Magnolia Creek residents?
Magnolia Creek Golf Club is a private club — membership is separate from HOA dues and is not automatically included with home ownership. Residents can choose to join for full access to the 27-hole course, Lone Pine Grill clubhouse dining, tennis, and fitness. Even non-members enjoy golf course views, green space settings, and proximity to the club's social scene, which is central to the neighborhood's lifestyle.
What is the price range for homes in Magnolia Creek?
Home prices range from approximately $345,000 for standard entry-level homes to $800,000 or more for premium golf course or lake-view properties. The most active market is the $420,000–$600,000 range, with median sale prices around $459,000–$490,000. For a private golf community with CCISD schools, Magnolia Creek offers strong value compared to similar-amenity communities elsewhere in the Houston area.
What builders built homes in Magnolia Creek?
Magnolia Creek was developed starting in the early 2000s by Perry Homes, Lennar, and K. Hovnanian Homes, among others. Development was largely complete by 2012–2016. The mix of builders created a variety of traditional and Mediterranean-influenced floor plans across multiple sections — giving the community genuine diversity in style, size, and price point.
How far is Magnolia Creek from downtown Houston?
Magnolia Creek is approximately 25–30 minutes from downtown Houston via I-45 North. Its west-side League City location makes I-45 access very straightforward. NASA Johnson Space Center is about 15 minutes away, UTMB League City is roughly 20 minutes, and Kemah Boardwalk is about 20 minutes.
What restaurants are near Magnolia Creek?
Residents have on-site dining at Lone Pine Grill inside the Magnolia Creek Golf Club. Nearby options include Legends Sports Grill and Red Oak Cafe (both at 6011 W Main St), Kabuki Japanese Grill & Sushi Bar (2525 Gulf Fwy S), Jimmy Changas (2504 S Gulf Fwy), The Toasted Yolk Cafe (2535 Gulf Fwy S), and Rudy's Country Store & Bar-B-Q (21361 Gulf Fwy, Webster).
What grocery stores are near Magnolia Creek?
H-E-B (2955 Gulf Fwy) is the closest and most popular for residents. Kroger (1920 W League City Pkwy) is also nearby with a full pharmacy and fuel center. CVS and Walgreens cover pharmacy and convenience needs. Most grocery runs take under 10 minutes from the community.
What amenities does Magnolia Creek have?
Magnolia Creek features a private 27-hole championship golf course, a clubhouse with Lone Pine Grill dining, tennis courts, and a fitness center (all via club membership). The broader community includes resort-style pools, playgrounds, parks, and walking trails. Golf course views, lakes, creeks, and green spaces are woven throughout the neighborhood, creating a resort-feel setting without requiring club membership to enjoy.
What is it like to live in Magnolia Creek?
Residents consistently describe Magnolia Creek as upscale, peaceful, and family-friendly — a golf and country club lifestyle that's genuinely integrated into the neighborhood rather than adjacent to it. The private course creates a beautiful, green backdrop throughout the community. For active residents, the club membership is a meaningful social investment. For families, Clear Springs High School and the full CCISD assignment are a major draw. The overall feel is sophisticated yet accessible — one of the best combinations of lifestyle and value on League City's west side.
