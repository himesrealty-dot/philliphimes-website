The Peacock Isle Experience

Waterway living in a scenic island-style setting — affordable Galveston County prices

"The name tells the story — it's an isle, surrounded by water. You're in Dickinson but the neighborhood has its own world, and once you're on the canal, Houston feels very far away."

Peacock Isle is one of Dickinson's most scenic waterway communities, with a canal network that gives the neighborhood a genuine island-like character. Streets wind around the water, and the homes that line the canals enjoy views and access that are rare at this price point anywhere in the Houston Bay Area.

Canal-front properties in Peacock Isle can accommodate boat docks, giving residents direct waterway access to fish, cruise the bayou, or reach broader bay connections. The community's established character — mature trees, lived-in homes, neighbors who chose this place on purpose — creates a sense of place that newer master-planned neighborhoods can't replicate.

Galveston County's lower tax rates amplify the value here. Buyers who might be priced out of waterfront access in Harris County can often find their waterway lifestyle in Peacock Isle at a fraction of the cost — while still being 35 minutes from downtown Houston and 18 minutes from Kemah's waterfront dining.

Common Questions

Peacock Isle — frequently asked questions

What school district is Peacock Isle in?
Peacock Isle is zoned to Dickinson Independent School District (DISD). The typical feeder pattern runs through Dunbar Middle School and Dickinson High School. The specific elementary campus can vary by street — always confirm before purchasing.
Does Peacock Isle have waterfront lots with canal access?
Yes — Peacock Isle has canal-front lots with access to the Dickinson waterway network. Canal-front homeowners can typically install boat docks for waterway access. Not all lots are canal-front — confirm water access for any specific address before purchasing.
What is the price range in Peacock Isle?
Home prices in Peacock Isle range from approximately $260,000 for interior lots to around $420,000 for canal-front properties. The most active price band is $310K–$370K for updated homes with varying water proximity.
How far is Peacock Isle from Galveston and Kemah?
Peacock Isle is approximately 22 minutes from Galveston Island via I-45 South and 18 minutes from Kemah Boardwalk — making both beach trips and waterfront dining easy regular outings.
What are the HOA fees in Peacock Isle?
Peacock Isle HOA fees are approximately $300–$500 per year. Always verify the current amount with the HOA or community association before closing.
How do Galveston County taxes compare to Harris County?
Galveston County property tax rates are generally lower than comparable Harris County addresses — often $1,000–$2,000 per year less on similarly priced homes. Always verify current rates with the Galveston Central Appraisal District.
