The Peacock Isle Experience

A small new-construction enclave with real frontage on Dickinson Bayou

"This isn't a decades-old subdivision dressed up as waterfront. It's a brand-new community where almost half the homesites back right onto Dickinson Bayou — and that frontage is the real thing."

Peacock Isle is a new-construction waterfront community off FM 517 in Dickinson, between I-45 and State Highway 3. It's small on purpose — just 50 homesites — and it's built by Bayway Homes, a builder that has focused on coastal and waterfront homes since 1997. The first homes went up in 2022, so everything here is new, and the sales center sits right in the community at 3921 Peacock Isle Dr.

What sets it apart is genuine water frontage. Of the 50 lots, 23 have direct backyard frontage on Dickinson Bayou — not a canal, not a manufactured pond, but the bayou itself. From those homes you can fish, kayak, canoe, or water-ski right off your own backyard, and there are two public watercraft launches close by: one where Highway 3 crosses the bayou, and another at Paul Hopkins Park on FM 517. The interior lots aren't waterfront, but the whole community shares the bayou setting.

There's a financial advantage worth calling out: Peacock Isle has no MUD, PID, or PUD tax. A lot of newer Houston-area communities carry one of those special-district levies on top of your property taxes, and they add up year after year. Skipping them here keeps the carrying cost lower — and you're still 35 minutes from downtown Houston, 22 from Galveston, and 18 from Kemah's waterfront.

Common Questions

Peacock Isle — frequently asked questions

Is Peacock Isle a new-construction community?
Yes — Peacock Isle is a new-construction waterfront community built from 2022 onward by Bayway Homes, a builder that has specialized in coastal and waterfront homes since 1997. It is a small enclave of 50 homesites off FM 517 in Dickinson, with a sales center at 3921 Peacock Isle Dr. Floor plans run 1,432 to 3,414 square feet in 3-, 4-, and 5-bedroom layouts.
Is Peacock Isle really on the water?
Yes — Peacock Isle is genuinely waterfront on Dickinson Bayou (not a canal off Galveston Bay). Of the 50 homesites, 23 have direct backyard frontage on Dickinson Bayou. From those lots residents can fish, kayak, canoe, and water-ski, and there are two public watercraft launches nearby — at Highway 3 where it crosses the bayou, and at Paul Hopkins Park on FM 517. Confirm the specific water frontage for any address before purchasing.
What is the price range in Peacock Isle?
Peacock Isle homes run roughly $340,000 to $760,000. Most homes land between $345,000 and $550,000, while premium direct-bayou-frontage lots reach into the $700s. Recent closed sales (December 2025 to June 2026) came in at $345K, $382K, $383K, $410K, and $548K, for a median of $383,000 — though that is a small sample of just five sales, so treat it as a rough guide rather than a firm benchmark.
How far is Peacock Isle from Galveston?
Peacock Isle is approximately 22 minutes from Galveston Island via I-45 South — convenient for beach trips and UTMB medical care.
What are the HOA fees in Peacock Isle?
HOA dues in Peacock Isle vary, so confirm the current amount for any specific home before closing. What is confirmed is that Peacock Isle has no MUD, PID, or PUD tax — which keeps the overall tax rate lower than many newer Houston-area communities that carry those special-district levies.
What are Galveston County taxes like?
Galveston County property tax rates are generally lower than comparable Harris County addresses — typically $1,000–$2,000 per year less on similarly priced homes. Peacock Isle also carries no MUD, PID, or PUD tax. Always verify current rates with the Galveston Central Appraisal District.
