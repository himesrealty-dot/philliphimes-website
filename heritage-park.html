The Heritage Park Experience

North Friendswood character — wooded lots, established streets, and lower entry prices

"Heritage Park is one of those neighborhoods that buyers discover when they want the Friendswood address without the master-planned price tag. Large wooded lots, quiet streets, and a genuine neighborhood feel — just note that this part of Friendswood is zoned to Clear Creek ISD."

Heritage Park represents the older, established character of north Friendswood. Developed in the 1970s and 1980s along the northern edge of the city near the League City border and FM 518, the neighborhood grew organically around wooded lots and natural terrain. The result is a community with a character distinctly different from newer master-planned subdivisions — larger lots, genuine tree coverage, and streets that feel lived-in.

Homes in Heritage Park are primarily single-story brick properties built in the 1970s through 1990s. Floor plans typically range from 1,600 to 2,800 sq ft, and many sit on lots that would cost twice as much to purchase in a newer development. The absence of significant HOA restrictions gives homeowners flexibility for additions, pools, outbuildings, and landscaping choices.

One important distinction: Heritage Park is zoned to Clear Creek ISD rather than Friendswood ISD. Buyers who specifically need FISD should confirm zoning before purchasing. However, Clear Creek ISD is itself a well-regarded district, and buyers who are flexible on the school district often find Heritage Park offers the best price-per-square-foot in the entire Friendswood ZIP code.

Common Questions

Heritage Park — frequently asked questions

What school district is Heritage Park Friendswood in?
Important: Heritage Park is zoned to Clear Creek ISD, not Friendswood ISD. This is the most critical thing buyers need to know before purchasing in this neighborhood. Clear Creek ISD is a well-regarded district, but buyers who require FISD zoning should confirm this before making an offer. Campus assignments are address-specific — always verify with CCISD.
What are home prices in Heritage Park Friendswood?
Heritage Park homes typically range from the low $300Ks for entry-level properties to $550K+ for larger, updated homes on premium lots. The most active range is $380K–$470K. Heritage Park consistently offers some of the lowest price-per-square-foot in the Friendswood ZIP code.
Does Heritage Park have an HOA?
Heritage Park has low or no HOA fees depending on the specific section. The absence of significant HOA restrictions is one of the neighborhood's most appealing features for buyers who want flexibility to add pools, buildings, or make exterior changes without association approval.
Why is Heritage Park less expensive than other Friendswood neighborhoods?
Heritage Park is priced lower than many Friendswood neighborhoods primarily because it is zoned to Clear Creek ISD rather than Friendswood ISD. The Friendswood ISD premium is significant — buyers who don't require FISD zoning can often buy a larger home on a bigger lot in Heritage Park for considerably less than they would pay in FISD-zoned communities.
How far is Heritage Park from downtown Houston?
Heritage Park is approximately 30 minutes from downtown Houston via I-45 North. NASA Johnson Space Center is about 20 minutes, the Texas Medical Center approximately 35 minutes, and Kemah Boardwalk about 25 minutes.
What restaurants are near Heritage Park Friendswood?
Heritage Park is a short drive from all of Friendswood's dining — B1895 Tavern and Locals Kitchen & Market, BJ’s Brewhouse, Fish City Grill, and the Bay Area Blvd corridor. Red Oak Cafe in League City is also close for casual breakfast and lunch.
Are there trails near Heritage Park?
Yes — Heritage Park Trail is practically in the neighborhood's backyard. This Friendswood trail system is one of the most popular outdoor amenities in the city, offering flat, shaded walking and biking paths through green space. Lake Friendswood Park is also nearby for fishing and trail access.
What kind of homes are in Heritage Park?
Heritage Park consists primarily of single-story and two-story brick homes built in the 1970s through 1990s. Floor plans range from approximately 1,600 to 2,800 sq ft. Lot sizes are generally larger than newer master-planned communities, and many homes have mature trees providing natural shade and privacy.
Is Heritage Park a good neighborhood to buy in?
Heritage Park is an excellent choice for buyers who want a Friendswood address and large wooded lot at a lower price point, and who are flexible on school district. The neighborhood offers genuine character, minimal HOA restrictions, and strong price-per-square-foot value. Buyers who need Friendswood ISD should verify their address zoning first.
What is Clear Creek ISD like?
Clear Creek ISD (CCISD) is a large, well-regarded school district serving the Clear Lake, Webster, and northern Friendswood areas. While it doesn't carry the same regional premium as FISD, CCISD has strong academic programs and is consistently rated above average. Specific school quality varies by campus — research your feeder schools before purchasing.
