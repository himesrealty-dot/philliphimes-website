Why You Need a REALTOR® for New Construction

The builder's on-site sales agent has one job: maximize the builder's profit. They're professionals at it. You need someone in your corner — and Phil's representation costs you nothing, because the builder pays the buyer's agent fee.

Phil has walked hundreds of new construction deals in South Houston. He knows which builders deliver on their promises, which upgrades are worth paying for, and what the contract language actually means when things go sideways during the build.

From your first model home visit through the final walk-through punch list, Phil protects your investment at every stage.

Common Questions

New Construction FAQ

Have a question about a specific builder, community, or lot? Phil responds to every inquiry — call, text, or email anytime.

Ask Phil →

Do I need a REALTOR® to buy new construction?

Yes — and it costs you nothing extra. Builder sales agents work for the builder, not you. Bringing Phil gives you independent representation to negotiate upgrades, review contracts, and protect your interests. The builder pays his fee, so you get expert guidance at zero additional cost to you.

What new construction communities are near League City TX?

Active builder communities include Legacy, Westland Ranch, and Coastal Point in League City (CCISD); Legacy and Friendswood Trails in Friendswood (FISD); Massey Oaks and Alexander in Pearland (Alvin ISD); Peacock Isle in Dickinson (Dickinson ISD); Toll Brothers at Midline in Webster/Clear Lake (CCISD); and Valencia, Pomona, Meridiana, Del Bello Lakes, and Avellino in Manvel (Alvin ISD). Prices range from the mid $250s to $1M+ depending on community and builder.

How much does new construction cost near League City TX?

New construction starts as low as the mid $250s at Avellino in Manvel (KB Home). Westland Ranch in League City starts from the $270s. Manvel and Pearland communities (Meridiana, Pomona, Massey Oaks) typically run from the low $300s to high $400s. League City CCISD communities (Legacy, Coastal Point) start in the high $300s to $400s. Friendswood FISD new construction starts from the $400s to $600s. Lot premiums, elevation selections, and design center upgrades all add to the base price.

How long does it take to build a new home?

Quick move-in spec homes can close in 30–60 days. Standard production builds take 4–6 months from contract. Semi-custom builds with your selections take 6–9 months. Custom homes on your lot run 10–14 months or more. Phil monitors your timeline and attends all key build milestones.

Can I negotiate on a new construction home?

Yes — builders rarely reduce base price but regularly offer incentives: free upgrades, closing cost contributions, preferred lender rate buydowns, or appliance packages. Leverage is highest at quarter-end when builders need to hit sales targets. Phil knows which builders are negotiating and when, and he has existing relationships that typically produce better outcomes than going in alone.
