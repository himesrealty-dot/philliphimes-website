The Friendswood Lakes Experience

Central Friendswood living with lakes, mature trees, and a neighborhood that actually feels like one

"Friendswood Lakes gives you something harder to find than you'd think — an established neighborhood with real canopy trees, community lakes, and Friendswood ISD schools at a price that doesn't require a seven-figure budget."

Friendswood Lakes is one of Friendswood's most livable established communities. Developed through the 1990s and into the 2000s, the neighborhood was built around a network of scenic community lakes and mature trees that give it a settled, shaded character that newer developments simply can't replicate. Streets here feel like streets — wide, quiet, and canopied.

Homes in Friendswood Lakes are predominantly resale, which means more square footage per dollar compared to new construction. Floor plans range from comfortable starter homes in the high $400Ks to larger family properties in the $600K–$700K range, while the gated Dominion at Friendswood Lakes estate section reaches $900K–$1.3M+. Most homes feature traditional layouts with mature landscaping, spacious lots, and solid construction built to last.

The central location along the South Friendswood Drive corridor puts residents close to everything that makes Friendswood great — B1895 Tavern, H-E-B, Gold's Gym, and Heritage Park Trail are all within minutes. Friendswood ISD schools round out the value proposition, making Friendswood Lakes an excellent choice for families who want space, character, and top schools without the premium price tag of newer master-planned communities.

Common Questions

Friendswood Lakes — frequently asked questions

What school district is Friendswood Lakes in?
Friendswood Lakes is zoned to Friendswood ISD — one of the top-rated school districts in the Houston Bay Area. The neighborhood typically feeds into Cline Elementary, Friendswood Intermediate, and Friendswood High School, though campus assignments are address-specific. Always verify zoning for your specific address before purchasing.
What are HOA fees in Friendswood Lakes?
HOA fees in Friendswood Lakes are generally in the range of $400–$800 per year, making it one of the more affordable HOA communities in Friendswood. Fees typically cover common area maintenance, lake upkeep, and community amenities. Verify the current amount with the HOA before closing.
What price range are homes in Friendswood Lakes?
Homes in Friendswood Lakes generally range from $470K for entry-level 3-bedroom homes to $1.3M+ in the gated Dominion at Friendswood Lakes estate section. Most homes trade in the $500K–$700K range, representing well-maintained 4-bedroom homes with mature landscaping, while estate homes in The Dominion reach $900K–$1.3M+.
Does Friendswood Lakes have a community pool?
Yes — Friendswood Lakes features a community pool and tennis courts as part of its amenity package. The neighborhood also has community lakes, green spaces, and walking paths throughout. The established landscaping and lake views are a major draw for buyers who prefer a more natural community feel.
How far is Friendswood Lakes from Houston?
Friendswood Lakes is approximately 30 minutes from downtown Houston via I-45 North. NASA Johnson Space Center is about 20 minutes, the Texas Medical Center approximately 35 minutes, and Kemah Boardwalk about 25 minutes.
What restaurants are near Friendswood Lakes?
Friendswood Lakes is centrally located near Friendswood's best dining — B1895 Tavern and Locals Kitchen & Market (607 S Friendswood Dr), Birraporetti's (400 W Parkwood), Perry's & Sons, and the Baybrook Mall corridor with Maggiano's, Yard House, and more.
What grocery stores are near Friendswood Lakes?
H-E-B (701 W Parkwood Ave) is the primary grocery destination for most residents. Kroger on North Friendswood Drive is also very convenient. Both offer full pharmacies, curbside pickup, and fuel centers.
Are there lakes in Friendswood Lakes?
Yes — community lakes are woven throughout the Friendswood Lakes neighborhood. These provide scenic views for many homes, green buffer space, and a peaceful community atmosphere. The lakes support catch-and-release fishing and attract local wildlife, contributing to the neighborhood's established natural character.
What type of homes are in Friendswood Lakes?
Friendswood Lakes consists primarily of resale single-family homes built in the 1990s through 2000s. Floor plans range from approximately 1,800 to 3,500+ sq ft. Homes feature traditional and transitional styles with brick and stone exteriors, mature landscaping, and larger lot sizes compared to newer master-planned communities.
Is Friendswood Lakes a good neighborhood to buy in?
Friendswood Lakes consistently offers strong value in the Friendswood market — more space and more mature landscaping per dollar compared to newer master-planned communities, combined with top-rated Friendswood ISD schools and a central location. It's a neighborhood buyers often discover late and regret not finding sooner.
