The Silverlake Experience

Central Pearland living anchored by a scenic lake — established, affordable, and Pearland ISD

"Silverlake is what central Pearland living should feel like — an established community with a real lake, mature trees, community amenities, and Pearland ISD schools at prices that make sense for families who want value without compromise."

Silverlake established itself as one of Pearland's signature communities through the 1990s and 2000s, growing around a scenic central lake that gives the neighborhood its defining character. The lake anchors community life, providing waterfront views for many homes, scenic walking trails, and a natural focal point that newer communities built from scratch simply cannot replicate.

Homes in Silverlake represent some of the best established-community value in Pearland. Resale properties range from comfortable 3-bedroom homes in the $300Ks to larger updated properties approaching $600K+, all with access to community pools, tennis courts, and the lake trail system. The neighborhood's central location near Broadway and Veterans Dr puts residents close to virtually everything Pearland offers.

Silverlake is zoned to Pearland ISD, feeding into Silverlake Elementary, Berry Miller Junior High, and Glenda Dawson High School. Pearland ISD is consistently rated one of the stronger school districts in the Greater Houston area and is one of the primary reasons families choose Pearland. For buyers who want Pearland ISD schools, an established community with genuine lake character, and strong value — Silverlake is one of the city's best options.

Common Questions

Silverlake — frequently asked questions

What school district is Silverlake Pearland in?
Silverlake is zoned to Pearland ISD — one of the most respected school districts in Greater Houston. The neighborhood feeds into Silverlake Elementary, Berry Miller Junior High, and Glenda Dawson High School. Campus assignments are address-specific — always confirm zoning before purchasing.
Does Silverlake have a lake?
Yes — Silverlake is built around a scenic community lake that serves as the neighborhood's centerpiece. The lake provides waterfront views for many homes, scenic walking and biking trails along the shoreline, and a natural gathering space for residents. Lakefront and lake-view lots typically command a premium.
What are HOA fees in Silverlake Pearland?
HOA fees in Silverlake are typically $400–$750 per year, covering community pool maintenance, lake upkeep, trail maintenance, and common area landscaping. Verify the current amount with the HOA before closing.
What are home prices in Silverlake Pearland?
Homes in Silverlake range from the low $300Ks for entry-level resale to $600K+ for larger lake-view properties. The most active range is $400K–$510K — 4-bedroom homes in good condition with community amenity access. Silverlake represents solid value for Pearland ISD buyers.
How far is Silverlake from Houston?
Silverlake is approximately 25 minutes from downtown Houston via Hwy 288 North. The Texas Medical Center is about 20 minutes, making Silverlake particularly popular with TMC professionals and families. Galveston is about 40 minutes south.
What restaurants are near Silverlake?
Silverlake is close to all of Pearland's best dining on Broadway — The Toasted Yolk, Fish City Grill, BJ's Brewhouse, Lupe Tortilla, and Killen's Texas BBQ are all within easy reach. The Broadway and Veterans Dr corridor offers dozens of additional options.
What makes Silverlake different from newer Pearland communities?
Silverlake offers the advantages of an established community — a real lake, mature trees, Pearland ISD schools, and a central location — at prices generally lower than newer master-planned developments. The community has genuine neighborhood character built over 20+ years that newer subdivisions are still developing.
What grocery stores are near Silverlake?
H-E-B (2811 E Broadway, Pearland) is the primary grocery destination. Kroger (3707 E Broadway) is also very convenient. Both offer full pharmacies, curbside pickup, and fuel centers.
Are there trails in Silverlake?
Yes — Silverlake has a trail system around the community lake and connecting to parks throughout the neighborhood. The lake trail is particularly popular for morning walks, jogging, and evening strolls. The central Pearland location also provides easy access to Centennial Park and Independence Park.
Is Silverlake a good place to buy in Pearland?
Silverlake consistently offers strong value for Pearland ISD buyers who want an established community with genuine lake character, community amenities, and a central location. The neighborhood has good resale history and attracts both families and professionals who want the Pearland ISD advantage at reasonable prices.
