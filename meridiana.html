The Meridiana Experience

Iowa Colony resort living — the amenity standard that other communities measure themselves against

"Meridiana delivers something that sounds impossible until you see it — a genuine resort experience in a suburban community. The lazy river, the exploration theme, the on-site school. Buyers who move here don't miss the city because the community brings the experience to them."

Meridiana launched in 2016 as a deliberate statement about what a master-planned community could be. Developed in Iowa Colony — just south of Pearland along Hwy 288 — the community was designed around an exploration and discovery theme, with amenities that go considerably beyond the standard pool-and-clubhouse formula. The centerpiece is a resort-grade amenity center featuring a lazy river, resort pools, a fitness center, and recreation facilities that residents consistently describe as the best in the Greater Houston area.

The community encompasses thousands of acres with multiple neighborhoods built by various national homebuilders including David Weekley, Perry Homes, Trendmaker, and others. Home prices start around $400K for entry-level plans and extend to $900K+ for premium lots and luxury floor plans. The breadth of builders and price points gives buyers genuine flexibility, and new construction continues in several phases.

Meridiana Elementary School is located within the community, providing elementary students with a walkable school option — an amenity that significantly enhances day-to-day family logistics. The community feeds into Rodeo Palms Junior High and Manvel High School within Alvin ISD. While the Iowa Colony location means a longer Houston commute than northern Pearland communities, residents consistently cite the community's amenities and quality of life as worth the trade-off.

Common Questions

Meridiana — frequently asked questions

What school district is Meridiana in?
Meridiana is zoned to Alvin ISD. The community includes on-site Meridiana Elementary School, with students then feeding into Rodeo Palms Junior High and Manvel High School. Note: Meridiana is in Iowa Colony and is Alvin ISD — not Pearland ISD. Confirm your specific address zoning before purchasing.
Does Meridiana have a lazy river?
Yes — Meridiana's amenity center features a lazy river along with resort pools, splash features, and a recreation village. The lazy river is one of the signature features that distinguishes Meridiana from other master-planned communities in the area.
What are HOA fees in Meridiana?
HOA fees in Meridiana are approximately $1,000–$2,000 per year, reflecting the community's premium amenity package including the lazy river, resort pools, fitness center, trail system, and common area maintenance. Verify the current amount with the HOA before closing.
What are home prices in Meridiana?
Meridiana homes range from approximately $400K for entry new construction to $900K+ for premium luxury floor plans on the best lots. The most active price range is $530K–$700K, representing 4–5 bedroom homes with upgraded finishes.
Is Meridiana in Pearland?
Meridiana is technically in Iowa Colony, a separate municipality adjacent to Pearland. It is in the Pearland/Iowa Colony area along Hwy 288 and is commonly referred to as part of the greater Pearland market. It is served by Alvin ISD, not Pearland ISD.
How far is Meridiana from the Texas Medical Center?
Meridiana is approximately 30 minutes from the Texas Medical Center via Hwy 288 North — a direct highway commute with no Beltway navigation. This makes Meridiana one of the most convenient communities in the area for TMC employees.
What builders are building in Meridiana?
Meridiana features homes from multiple national builders including David Weekley Homes, Perry Homes, Trendmaker Homes, and others. The variety of builders creates different architectural styles and floor plan options across the community's price range.
Are there nature trails in Meridiana?
Yes — Meridiana's community design emphasizes a discovery and exploration theme with nature trails winding through the community. The trails connect to parks, natural areas, and amenity centers throughout the development, creating a genuinely outdoor-oriented community experience.
What restaurants are near Meridiana?
Meridiana is a short drive north on Hwy 288 from Pearland's restaurant corridor — Lupe Tortilla, BJ's Brewhouse, Killen's Texas BBQ, and Gringo's Mexican Kitchen are all accessible. Houston's restaurant scene is also 30–35 minutes north on the same highway.
Is Meridiana worth the longer commute?
Many Meridiana residents say yes — the community's resort amenities, modern homes, on-site school, and community design quality are a trade-off they make intentionally. For TMC professionals, the Hwy 288 commute is direct and manageable. The community consistently attracts buyers who prioritize lifestyle quality over proximity.
