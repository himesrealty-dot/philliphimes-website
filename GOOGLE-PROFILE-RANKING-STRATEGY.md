## How Google actually ranks a profile (the only 3 factors)
1. **Relevance** — how well your profile matches the search. Driven by: **primary category**, description, services, the city/keywords in your **reviews**, and your **website content**.
2. **Distance** — proximity of the searcher to your business location. You're a **service-area business anchored in League City**, so you'll always rank **strongest in/near League City** and weaker the farther out you go (Manvel is the hardest). This is largely uncontrollable — which is why we lean on the other two.
3. **Prominence** — how well-known/trusted you are. Driven by: **review count + velocity**, **NAP consistency across the web**, **backlinks**, and overall web presence.

**Translation:** You can't change distance. So the whole strategy is **maximize Relevance + Prominence** — and for a real estate agent, that means **reviews above everything**, then consistency, then completeness.

## PRIORITY 1 — A Review Engine (your single biggest lever) 🔴

You have 9 Google reviews; a top competitor has 41. Reviews are the #1 prominence factor **and** they feed relevance when reviewers mention the city/service. Close this gap and everything moves.

**Targets:** 25 Google reviews in 90 days → 50 by year-end. Aim for **2–4 new reviews/month minimum** (velocity matters as much as total).

**Tactics:**
- **Get your one-tap review link** from the GBP dashboard ("Ask for reviews" / "Get more reviews" → a `g.page/r/…` short link). Use it everywhere.
- **Ask every single closing** — at the closing table is best (highest emotion). In person > text > email for conversion.
- **Make it a system, not a hope:** text the review link the day of closing with a one-line ask + a thank-you. Follow up once 3 days later if no response.
- **Coach the content (this is the SEO multiplier):** ask happy clients to mention **(a) the city** and **(b) what you did** — e.g., *"Phil helped us buy our first home in League City."* City + service keywords in reviews directly boost relevance for those searches.
- **Mine your back catalog:** you have glowing reviews on Zillow & RealSatisfied (5.0 across the board). Reach back out to those past clients and ask them to copy it onto Google. That's the fastest 10–15 reviews you'll ever get.
- **Respond to every review within 24–48h** (you already do this well — keep it up; work the city name into your replies naturally).
- **Never buy/incentivize reviews** — against Google policy and risks suspension.
