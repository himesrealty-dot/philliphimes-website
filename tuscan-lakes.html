The Tuscan Lakes Experience

You know you've found something special before you ever park the car

"Entering Tuscan Lakes is a grand experience — the Roman aqueduct greets you, and as you drive through the community, the landscaping truly transports you to a Tuscany village."

Most neighborhoods in the Houston area look like neighborhoods. Tuscan Lakes looks like a destination. The Italian-themed design runs deep — from the tile-roofed homes in the gated section to the lavender blossom trees lining the streets, to flowers planted fresh three times a year. The HOA takes the aesthetic seriously, and it shows.

Every morning and evening you'll find residents walking the lake paths. It's that kind of community — one where people actually use the amenities, know their neighbors, and choose to stay. The lakes aren't decorative. Residents fish, paddleboard, and take out small non-motorized boats. The resort-style pool and trails mean you genuinely don't need to leave to decompress.

The gated section takes the experience up another level — luxury homes with modern floor plans, dramatic architecture, and a level of privacy and finish that competes with anywhere in the Houston Bay Area.

Schools

The school district depends on where in Tuscan Lakes you live

This is one of the most important things to understand about Tuscan Lakes — and one of the details most sites get wrong. Highway 96 splits the community between two school districts.

Clear Creek ISD (CCISD)
Homes north of Highway 96
Clear Creek ISD is one of the largest and most highly rated school districts in the Houston Bay Area, serving League City, Friendswood, Clear Lake, and Webster. Multiple campuses hold exemplary ratings.
Dickinson ISD
Homes south of Highway 96
Dickinson ISD serves the southern portion of League City and Dickinson. Always verify your specific address assignment before purchasing — the same street can feed different districts depending on the lot.
⚠️ Always verify before you buy. School assignments are address-specific. Ask Phil to confirm the exact school zoning for any home you're interested in before making an offer.

Common Questions

Tuscan Lakes — frequently asked questions

What school district is Tuscan Lakes in?
Tuscan Lakes is split between two school districts. Homes north of Highway 96 are served by Clear Creek ISD (CCISD). Homes south of Highway 96 fall under Dickinson ISD. The district assignment is address-specific — always verify before purchasing.
What are the HOA fees in Tuscan Lakes?
Tuscan Lakes HOA fees are approximately $750 per year. This covers the community's landscaping maintenance, the resort-style pool, walking trails, lakes, and the signature entrance with its iconic Roman aqueduct feature. Fees are subject to change — always confirm the current amount with the HOA before closing.
Can you fish in Tuscan Lakes?
Yes — the lakes in Tuscan Lakes are active recreational amenities. Residents fish, paddleboard, and use small non-motorized watercraft. The lakeside paths are popular for morning and evening walks, and the lakes are a central part of what makes the neighborhood feel like a resort community.
What builders built homes in Tuscan Lakes?
Tuscan Lakes was developed by five builders: Westin Homes, DR Horton, Wilshire Homes, Del Webb, and CastleRock Communities. Each builder has contributed different home styles and price points across the community, from entry-level production homes to luxury custom builds in the gated section.
How far is Tuscan Lakes from downtown Houston?
Tuscan Lakes is approximately 35 minutes from downtown Houston via I-45 North. It is also about 35 minutes from Galveston's beaches heading south. For most commuters — including those heading to NASA, UTMB, or the Texas City/Baytown petrochemical corridor — Tuscan Lakes sits within a 15–30 minute drive.
What is the price range for homes in Tuscan Lakes?
Home prices in Tuscan Lakes range from approximately $315,000 at the entry level, with a median sale price around $395,000 — most homes close between $330,000 and $470,000. The gated luxury section features waterfront and custom homes with recent sales from $905,000 to $1.375 million and current listings reaching $1.55 million, with Italian-inspired architecture, tile roofs, modern floor plans, and larger lots. The community offers genuine price diversity for buyers at different stages of life.
What restaurants are near Tuscan Lakes League City TX?
Several popular restaurants are within minutes of Tuscan Lakes. Nearby dining includes Craft 96 Draught House + Kitchen (2575 E League City Pkwy), Lupe Tortilla (5165 TX-96), Ocean Sushi & Grill (3020 Marina Bay Dr), The Spot (2502 Gulf Fwy S), and Kings Biergarten (828 FM 646 Rd, Dickinson). The Shops at Tuscan Lakes commercial corridor sits right at the community entrance — most dining is a 5-minute drive without getting on I-45.
What grocery stores are near Tuscan Lakes?
H-E-B (2755 E League City Pkwy) and Kroger (2750 E League City Pkwy) are both located on East League City Parkway — within a half-mile of each other and just minutes from the Tuscan Lakes entrance. CVS Pharmacy (1295 E League City Pkwy) and Walgreens (2585 E League City Pkwy) cover pharmacy and convenience needs. For daily grocery runs, Tuscan Lakes residents rarely need to travel more than 5 minutes.
What is the closest hospital to Tuscan Lakes League City?
The closest major hospital to Tuscan Lakes is UTMB Health League City Campus at 2240 Gulf Fwy S — approximately 10 minutes away. It is a full University of Texas Medical Branch hospital with a Level II trauma center, 24-hour emergency services, and a broad range of specialists. It is also one of League City's largest employers. Kelsey-Seybold Clinic (3625 E League City Pkwy) provides multi-specialty outpatient care nearby.
What is there to do near Tuscan Lakes in League City?
Tuscan Lakes is positioned between some of the Houston Bay Area's best attractions. Kemah Boardwalk (215 Kipp Ave, Kemah) is about 20 minutes away with waterfront dining and rides. Space Center Houston (1601 NASA Pkwy) is just 15 minutes north — a world-class destination that surprises out-of-town guests every time. Great Wolf Lodge indoor water park is in nearby Webster. Moody Gardens (aquarium, rainforest, IMAX) is 35 minutes south in Galveston. And Lagoon Fest is an annual community favorite held right in League City.
Is Tuscan Lakes a walkable neighborhood?
Tuscan Lakes is car-dependent for most errands — typical of suburban South Houston. That said, the Shops at Tuscan Lakes commercial center sits directly at the community entrance on League City Parkway, putting H-E-B, Kroger, restaurants, a gym, pharmacy, and banking within a 2–3 minute drive without touching I-45. Inside the neighborhood, well-maintained walking and jogging trails wind around the lakes and are popular morning and evening. Most residents consider the internal walkability a major quality-of-life feature, even if the broader area requires a car.
