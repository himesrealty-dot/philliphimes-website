About the District

Pearland ISD — The Original Pearland

Pearland ISD has served the Pearland community for over a century — long before the SH 288 corridor transformed the city into one of the fastest-growing suburbs in the nation. While newer developments along SH 288 feed into Alvin ISD, Pearland ISD serves the city's established core: walkable streets, mature trees, and neighborhoods with real character and history.

Buyers who prioritize lot size, tree canopy, proximity to original Pearland amenities, and a true neighborhood feel consistently look at Pearland ISD. Homes here offer solid value — often larger lots and more square footage than comparably priced new construction, without HOA fees common in newer communities.

Phil knows both Pearland ISD and Alvin ISD well. If you're weighing established Pearland versus the newer SH 288 corridor, he can walk you through the real tradeoffs — not just the school ratings.

📍 Why Buyers Choose Pearland ISD
  • Established neighborhoods with mature trees and larger lots
  • Often lower or no HOA fees compared to newer master-planned communities
  • Pearland HS — a community anchor with deep athletic and academic traditions
  • Dawson HS — strong academics serving eastern and growing Pearland areas
  • Central Pearland location — close to Beltway 8, SH 35, and downtown Pearland
  • Strong resale history in established neighborhoods
  • More character per dollar vs. tract-home subdivisions

Two High Schools. One Strong District.

Pearland ISD's two high schools serve distinct geographic zones. Phil confirms which campus serves every address before you make an offer.

Pearland High School
West & Central Pearland · Pearland ISD ⭐ Long-Standing Community School

The original Pearland high school — established campus with strong athletic traditions, a dedicated community following, and expanding academic programs. Serves the city's western and central established neighborhoods.

Serves: Southgate, Country Place, and established Pearland neighborhoods west of SH 35.
Glenda Dawson High School
East Pearland · Pearland ISD ⭐ Strong Academics

Opened in 2004 to accommodate Pearland's eastward growth. Named for longtime Pearland ISD educator Glenda Dawson — strong academic performance and a growing profile for both athletics and fine arts.

Serves: Eastern Pearland neighborhoods and areas near FM 518 and SH 35.

Common Questions

Pearland ISD Home Buying FAQ

Questions about school zones, neighborhood boundaries, or the Pearland ISD vs. Alvin ISD decision? Phil knows both districts cold.

Ask Phil →

What neighborhoods are in Pearland ISD?

Pearland ISD serves the older, established parts of Pearland — primarily neighborhoods west and south of SH 35, including Southgate and Country Place. Note: the newer SH 288 corridor communities (Shadow Creek Ranch, Silverlake, Riverstone Ranch, Southern Trails) are zoned to Alvin ISD, not Pearland ISD.

What is the difference between Pearland ISD and Alvin ISD?

Pearland ISD serves the original, established Pearland — older homes, larger lots, mature trees, and lower or no HOA fees. Alvin ISD serves the fast-growing SH 288 corridor — newer master-planned communities with resort amenities. Both are well-regarded. The right choice depends on what you prioritize: established character vs. newer construction.

Which high school serves Pearland ISD neighborhoods?

Pearland ISD has two high schools: Pearland High School (western and central neighborhoods) and Glenda Dawson High School (eastern areas). Which one serves your specific address depends on the boundary lines, which Phil verifies for every home you seriously consider.

What is the average home price in Pearland ISD?

Pearland ISD homes in our market typically range from $260K in Southgate to $420K in Country Place and nearby neighborhoods. These are established homes — buyers often get more lot size and mature landscaping than equivalent new construction at similar prices.

Is Pearland ISD a good school district?

Yes — Pearland ISD has served the community for over a century and maintains strong academic and athletic programs across both high schools. It's a district with deep community roots and stable leadership. TEA ratings and campus-specific performance data are worth reviewing with Phil before any purchase decision.
