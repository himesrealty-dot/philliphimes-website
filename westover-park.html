The Westover Park Experience

It feels like a neighborhood — because it was built to be one

"Westover Park has that rare quality you can't manufacture — the kind of place where neighbors know each other by name, kids ride bikes to the pool, and the trails actually get used every single morning."

There are master-planned communities that look great in a brochure, and there are ones that actually deliver on the promise. Westover Park has had nearly two decades to prove itself — and residents keep staying. The rotundas, the mature landscaping, the sidewalks that connect everything — it's designed around people, not cars.

Two full recreation centers and two swimming pools mean you're never far from amenities. The tennis complex, sports fields, and playgrounds give families an outlet year-round. The neighborhood parks, lakes, and walking trails weave through the community in a way that makes an evening walk feel genuinely restorative rather than just exercise.

Westover Park hits a sweet spot: the quality of an established master-planned community with a price point that's still attainable for move-up buyers. Top CCISD schools, easy access to I-45, and a location that puts you close to everything in League City without being in the middle of it all.

Common Questions

Westover Park — frequently asked questions

What school district is Westover Park in?
Westover Park is fully within Clear Creek ISD (CCISD) — there is no split district to worry about. Every home in the community feeds into Florence Campbell Elementary, Creekside Intermediate, and Clear Springs High School. CCISD is consistently rated one of the top school districts in the Houston Bay Area. Confirm the specific assignment for any address before purchasing.
What are the HOA fees in Westover Park?
Westover Park HOA fees are approximately $728 per year. This covers the community's two recreation centers, two swimming pools, sports fields, tennis complex, playgrounds, neighborhood parks, lakes, and walking trails. Fees are subject to change — always verify the current amount with the HOA before closing.
What builders built homes in Westover Park?
Westover Park was developed by five builders: NewMark Homes, D.R. Horton, Taylor Morrison, David Weekley, and Perry Homes. Each builder brought different home styles and price points — from entry-level production homes to larger, custom-finish properties. The variety means buyers can find something that fits both their budget and their taste within the same community.
How far is Westover Park from downtown Houston?
Westover Park is approximately 30–35 minutes from downtown Houston via I-45 North. The community sits on the south side of SH 518 about 3 miles west of I-45, giving convenient access to the freeway without the noise or congestion of living directly on it. Most residents heading to NASA, UTMB, or the Texas City/Baytown petrochemical corridor are within 12–30 minutes.
What is the price range for homes in Westover Park?
Home prices in Westover Park currently range from approximately $335,000 at the entry level to $600,000+ for larger, higher-finish homes, with a median sale price around $415,000. The most active price band is $360K–$500K, where buyers typically find 2,200–3,000 sq ft homes with updated finishes and full community access. Compared to some nearby master-planned communities, Westover Park offers strong value for the schools and amenities you get.
What restaurants are near Westover Park in League City?
Several popular restaurants are within minutes of Westover Park. Nearby dining includes Legends Sports Grill (6011 W Main St), Red Oak Cafe (6011 W Main St — local breakfast favorite), Kabuki Japanese Grill & Sushi Bar (2525 Gulf Fwy S), Jimmy Changas (2504 S Gulf Fwy), The Toasted Yolk Cafe (2535 Gulf Fwy S), and Rudy's Country Store & Bar-B-Q (21361 Gulf Fwy, Webster). Most are along SH 518 or the Gulf Freeway corridor — a 5–10 minute drive.
What grocery stores are near Westover Park?
H-E-B (2955 Gulf Fwy, League City) and Kroger (1920 W League City Pkwy) are the two primary grocery options near Westover Park. CVS Pharmacy (1295 E League City Pkwy) and Walgreens (2585 E League City Pkwy) cover pharmacy and convenience needs. Daily grocery runs are typically under 10 minutes from the neighborhood.
What is the closest hospital to Westover Park?
The closest major hospital to Westover Park is UTMB Health League City Campus at 2240 Gulf Fwy S — approximately 10–15 minutes away. It is a full University of Texas Medical Branch facility with a Level II trauma center, 24-hour emergency services, and specialists across a wide range of disciplines. It is also one of League City's largest employers. Kelsey-Seybold Clinic (3625 E League City Pkwy) handles multi-specialty outpatient care nearby.
What amenities does Westover Park have?
Westover Park features two full recreation centers, two swimming pools (including options available for private parties), a tennis complex, sports fields, playgrounds, and numerous neighborhood parks, lakes, and walking trails. The community was designed to be genuinely livable — with amenities residents use daily, not just featured in marketing materials.
What is it like to live in Westover Park?
Westover Park has developed a strong, cohesive community identity over nearly two decades. Residents consistently describe it as friendly, active, and convenient — with mature landscaping, well-maintained amenities, rotundas and sidewalks that encourage neighbors to be outside, and a genuine sense of belonging. It draws families for the CCISD schools, professionals for the commute access, and anyone looking for strong value within a master-planned environment.
Is Westover Park a walkable neighborhood?
Westover Park is car-dependent for most errands — typical of suburban South Houston. Inside the community, well-maintained walking trails wind around the parks and lakes and are popular with residents morning and evening. For daily needs, H-E-B and Kroger are within a short drive, and the Gulf Freeway corridor puts dining, shopping, and services conveniently close. Most residents consider the internal walkability a quality-of-life feature, even if the broader area requires a car.
